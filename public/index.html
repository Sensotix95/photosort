Frequently Asked Questions

Do my photos get uploaded to the cloud?

No. Your photos never leave your computer. The AI classification model runs directly in your browser. Only anonymized metadata (dates, location names) is sent for event naming — your actual photo files stay on your device.

Will my original photos be deleted or moved?

Never. SortMyPics copies your photos into the new organized structure. Your originals are untouched. You review the full proposed folder tree before anything is copied.

Can I sort photos by date automatically?

Yes. SortMyPics reads EXIF date metadata from your photos and creates Year/Month folder structures automatically. It also detects named events within each time period.

What photo formats are supported?

SortMyPics supports JPEG, PNG, HEIC, WebP, and TIFF images, plus MP4, MOV, AVI, MKV, and M4V videos.

Which browsers work with SortMyPics?

SortMyPics requires Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge (version 86+). It uses the File System Access API to read and write files locally. Firefox does not yet support this API.

Is this a subscription or one-time payment?

No monthly fees, no annual renewal, no account required. See the pricing page for details.

How is SortMyPics different from Google Photos?

Google Photos uploads your photos to Google's servers. SortMyPics keeps everything on your computer. You also get a real folder structure you own — not just albums in an app. See our full comparison with Google Photos.

How does the AI detect trips and events?

SortMyPics combines GPS metadata, timestamps, and visual content analysis (CLIP AI) to group photos into sessions. It detects when you were away from home, classifies events by content (weddings, concerts, outdoor activities), and uses Gemini AI to suggest meaningful folder names.
